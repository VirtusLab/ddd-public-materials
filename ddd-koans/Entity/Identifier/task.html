Identifier

In the previous parts of the course, the identification topic was briefly mentioned. The value object that is identified and differentiated by its value as described but what are other possibilities to differentiate between concepts?

The domain concept not identified by its values is called Entity in DDD. Any entity represents more than just simple data container, it has its identity and must be identifiable somehow. Here comes the concept of the identifier.

How can you identify a person? You might have guessed that with the given name and surname, but are you sure about that? People sometimes share given names and surnames. So governments around the world bestow multi-character codes upon every citizen to make the citizens distinguishable. It is sometimes your id card number or insurance number.

The described above identifier is of artificial identifier type. The numbers are not directly connected with the entities, however, now they are distinguishable. They are typically generated by your software. Popular examples are UUIDs or sequential ids get from a database.

Yet, natural identifiers also exist. A page in the book is identified by its unique number. Each day is identified by year, month and day in a month - a combination of all those attributes makes each day unique and identifiable. These kind of identifiers are typically provided externally, for example by user.

Notes about identifier

  • One identifier for one entity. It cannot change overtime.
  • It can be represented as a value object.
  • It can be one or more values, as long as it ensures uniqueness of entity.
